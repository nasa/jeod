79 /**
80  * An EphemerisRefFrame is a RefFrame whose state is set by an ephemeris model.
81  * Ephemeris reference frames come in three basic flavors:
82  * - Planet-centered inertial frames. These are non-rotating frames whose
83  * origin is the center of mass of some planet (the Sun is a planet)
84  * and whose translational motion is (for now) assumed to be due to
85  * gravitation only. The orientation with respect to inertial is the
86  * identity transformation.
87  * - Barycenter inertial frames. These are non-rotating frames whose origin
88  * is the center of mass of two or more planets.
89  * - Planet-fixed frames. These are rotating frames whose origin is the
90  * center of mass of some planet (see ISSUE) and that rotate with the
91  * planet in question. The parent is always a planet-centered inertial
92  * frame with a zero translation offset between the planet-centered
93  * inertial and planet-fixed frames.
94  *
95  * Only planet-centered inertial and barycenter inertial frames can serve
96  * as integration frames or as the root of the reference frame tree.
97  */
